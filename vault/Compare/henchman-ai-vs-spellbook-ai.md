# Henchman AI vs Spellbook AI: Which Drafting Assistant Wins

Henchman AI and Spellbook AI both live inside Microsoft Word, but they attack contract drafting from different angles. Henchman surfaces your firm's own precedent clauses as you draft, while Spellbook generates AI-suggested language benchmarked against six million public contracts. Both claim to make drafting faster; the difference lies in where the intelligence comes from.

## Quick Verdict

Henchman AI is the right choice for law firms with rich precedent libraries in their document management system, who want to surface and reuse firm knowledge. Spellbook AI is the right choice for firms and solos who want generative drafting suggestions backed by market benchmarks. Henchman leverages your firm's memory; Spellbook leverages market data.

## When to Choose Henchman AI

Henchman AI is the right pick for established law firms with well-organized precedent libraries in iManage or NetDocuments. Henchman indexes your firm's past deals and lets associates search precedent clauses directly inside Word, pulling the exact language partners prefer. It is especially valuable for firms with strong firm knowledge but unorganized precedent retrieval.

## When to Choose Spellbook AI

Spellbook AI is the right pick for firms and solos that want generative drafting backed by market benchmarks. Spellbook writes new clauses, redlines counterparty drafts, and tells you whether a clause is market-standard by comparing it against its six-million-contract corpus. Spellbook is faster to deploy because it does not depend on DMS indexing.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Henchman AI** uses enterprise pricing typically around $90 per user per month with implementation fees for DMS integration. **Spellbook AI** starts at $89 per user per month with no implementation fees. Both offer free trials.

## Final Recommendation

For mid to large law firms with well-maintained precedent libraries, Henchman AI is the better investment because it monetizes existing firm knowledge. For solos, small firms, and in-house teams that want fast AI-assisted drafting, Spellbook AI is the stronger choice. Some large firms license both, using Henchman for partner-driven drafting and Spellbook for associate-driven drafting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Henchman require iManage?**
Henchman supports iManage, NetDocuments, and SharePoint. Without one of these, its value drops significantly.

**Can Spellbook search my firm's precedent?**
No. Spellbook relies on its public benchmark corpus and LLM generation, not firm-specific data.

**Which is faster to deploy?**
Spellbook deploys in minutes. Henchman typically takes 4-8 weeks due to DMS indexing.

**Which has better multilingual support?**
Both support multiple languages. Henchman is stronger in European languages; Spellbook is stronger in Spanish.

**Can I use both?**
Yes, many large firms deploy both: Henchman for precedent retrieval and Spellbook for generative drafting.
