# Kira Systems vs Spellbook AI: Contract Review vs Contract Drafting

Kira Systems and Spellbook AI occupy different corners of the contract lifecycle, but they are often compared because both serve transactional lawyers. Kira extracts and reviews clauses from existing documents at scale, while Spellbook drafts and edits new contracts directly inside Microsoft Word using GPT-class models. Understanding which problem you are actually solving is the first step.

## Quick Verdict

If your pain point is reviewing inbound contracts at scale, Kira Systems is the right tool. If your pain point is drafting and negotiating contracts faster inside Word, Spellbook AI is the right tool. Many firms end up with both, using Kira for due diligence and Spellbook for day-to-day drafting.

## When to Choose Kira Systems

Choose Kira Systems when your bottleneck is reviewing thousands of contracts during an M&A transaction, real estate portfolio acquisition, or large-scale diligence project. Kira's strength is extraction: pull every change-of-control provision, every assignment clause, every termination right out of a data room in hours instead of weeks.

## When to Choose Spellbook AI

Choose Spellbook AI when your bottleneck is drafting. Spellbook lives inside Word and offers inline AI suggestions as you draft. It can review a counterparty's markup, suggest redlines aligned with your playbook, and benchmark unusual clauses against its corpus of six million contracts to tell you whether a provision is market-standard. Solo transactional lawyers and small in-house teams love it.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Kira Systems** uses enterprise pricing with multi-year contracts, typically starting around $40,000 per year and scaling into six figures. **Spellbook AI** uses per-seat subscriptions starting at $89 per user per month, with higher tiers for additional features and API access. A 20-lawyer corporate group can deploy Spellbook for under $25,000 per year.

## Kira Systems Pros and Cons

**Pros:**

- Mature reporting and analytics

**Cons:**
- Expensive and complex
- Does not draft or redline

## Spellbook AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**

- Transparent per-seat pricing
- No training required
- Market benchmarking built in
- Instant onboarding

**Cons:**
- Not designed for large data room review
- Word-only (no web app)
- LLM hallucination risk on niche clauses
- Limited reporting
- Smaller enterprise footprint

## Final Recommendation

These tools solve different problems, so the right answer is usually both. If forced to choose one, small and mid-sized firms should pick Spellbook AI because drafting efficiency compounds daily. Large firms with heavy M&A flow should pick Kira Systems because due diligence economics demand it. Evaluate your actual workflow bottleneck before deciding.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Spellbook review large data rooms?**
Not efficiently. Spellbook is optimized for single-contract drafting, not bulk extraction.

**Does Kira draft contracts?**
No. Kira reviews and extracts but does not generate new contract language.

**Is Spellbook safe for confidential contracts?**
Yes. Spellbook operates under a zero-retention agreement with its LLM provider and is SOC 2 Type II compliant.

**Which integrates with iManage?**
Kira has deeper iManage integration. Spellbook integrates with Word but relies on Word's iManage plugin.

**Which is easier to adopt?**
Spellbook wins on adoption. Most associates start using it within minutes of installation.
