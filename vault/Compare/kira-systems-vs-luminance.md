# Kira Systems vs Luminance: Which Contract Review AI Wins

Kira Systems and Luminance pioneered AI-powered contract review and remain the two most widely deployed platforms for M&A due diligence and large-scale document analysis. Each uses machine learning to extract clauses, flag risks, and accelerate reviews. Their philosophies diverge on training data, UI, and deployment model. This guide breaks down the practical differences.

## Quick Verdict

Kira Systems is the better choice for firms that want a mature, battle-tested platform with a massive pre-built clause library. Luminance is the stronger pick for firms that want unsupervised learning that adapts to novel document types without manual training. Both are enterprise-priced; the right choice depends on your deal volume and desire for customization.

## When to Choose Kira Systems

Kira Systems is the right call for firms with high M&A and real estate deal volume. Its 1,400+ pre-built clause models cover virtually every provision you will encounter in US and UK deals. Kira is also deeply integrated with the Litera document lifecycle suite, making it the natural choice for firms already using Contract Companion, DocXtools, or Clause Companion.

## When to Choose Luminance

Luminance is the right call for global firms handling cross-border deals in multiple languages. Its unsupervised learning engine adapts to novel document types faster than Kira, making it especially useful for one-off transactions where training a supervised model is not practical. Luminance's visual cluster analysis is also a favorite of litigators handling large early case assessment projects.

## Pricing Breakdown

Both platforms use enterprise pricing with multi-year commitments. **Kira Systems** pricing typically starts around $40,000 per year for a small team and scales to six figures for firm-wide deployments. **Luminance** pricing starts around $35,000 per year with similar scaling. Actual quotes depend heavily on document volume, number of users, and add-on modules.

## Kira Systems Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Massive pre-built clause library
- Deep Litera ecosystem integration
- Battle-tested on AmLaw M&A deals
- Mature reporting and analytics
- Strong vendor support

**Cons:**
- Supervised models require training effort
- Less flexible on novel document types
- Higher all-in cost at scale
- Dated UI in places
- Slower to add new languages

## Luminance Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Unsupervised learning adapts fast
- 80+ language support
- Strong visual analytics
- Competitive pricing
- Good for litigation ECA

**Cons:**
- Smaller pre-built library
- Less opinionated workflows
- Third-party integrations less mature
- Steeper learning curve on analytics
- Requires more manual review on edge cases

## Final Recommendation

For US-focused M&A practices, Kira Systems is the proven choice with the deepest clause library and tightest Litera integration. For global firms handling multi-lingual deals or litigation ECA, Luminance is the more flexible and future-proof option. Firms with both needs sometimes license both platforms for different practice groups.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Kira handle non-English contracts?**
Yes, Kira supports 40+ languages but its pre-built models are strongest in English.

**Does Luminance require training?**
Minimal training is required for standard document types. Custom work still benefits from targeted training sessions.

**Which is faster on large data rooms?**
Both can process thousands of documents per hour. Luminance's unsupervised engine is typically faster on heterogeneous document sets.

**Which integrates better with iManage?**
Kira has deeper iManage integration thanks to Litera's relationship with iManage.

**Can either be used for in-house contract review?**
Yes, both platforms serve corporate legal departments, though most in-house teams find them overkill compared to Evisort or Ironclad.
