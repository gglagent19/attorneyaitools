# Spellbook AI vs LegalOn: Which Contract AI Wins for Drafting and Review

Spellbook AI and LegalOn are two of the fastest growing contract AI tools of 2026. Both live inside Microsoft Word, both use large language models to speed up drafting and review, and both target busy transactional lawyers. Under the hood, however, they take different approaches. This comparison clarifies which is the better fit for your workflow.

## Quick Verdict

Spellbook AI leads on raw drafting power and market benchmarking, making it the favorite of corporate lawyers and solos. LegalOn leads on structured playbook review and enterprise compliance, making it the favorite of in-house teams managing high inbound contract volume. Pick based on whether you draft more or review more.

## When to Choose Spellbook AI

Spellbook AI is the right pick for outside counsel who draft and negotiate contracts across a wide range of transactions. Spellbook's killer feature is its benchmark database of six million contracts, which lets lawyers compare clause language against market norms in seconds. Corporate boutiques and M&A associates love this for negotiation leverage.

## When to Choose LegalOn

LegalOn is the right pick for in-house legal teams that process high volumes of inbound contracts against company playbooks. LegalOn's strength is structured review: feed it your playbook, drop in a counterparty draft, and it returns suggested redlines aligned with your positions. LegalOn's Japanese origins also mean it has strong Japanese language support, valuable for companies with APAC operations.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Spellbook AI** starts at $89 per user per month for the core plan and scales to $129 for Pro which adds benchmark analytics and API access. Enterprise pricing is available for teams over 50 seats.

**LegalOn** starts at $99 per user per month with enterprise pricing available at volume. LegalOn also offers a dedicated in-house plan bundled with workflow management.

## Final Recommendation

Outside counsel and solos should start with Spellbook AI for its drafting power and market benchmarks. In-house legal teams should start with LegalOn for its playbook discipline. Companies with both needs can license Spellbook for lawyers who draft and LegalOn for those who triage inbound contracts, though most teams pick one to standardize training.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Spellbook replace my contract review process entirely?**
No. Spellbook accelerates drafting and review but still requires human judgment on material terms.

**Does LegalOn support custom playbooks?**
Yes, LegalOn ships with pre-built playbooks and supports unlimited custom playbook creation.

**Which is better for NDAs?**
Both excel on NDAs. LegalOn's playbook approach is slightly faster for high-volume NDA triage.

**Can Spellbook be used by non-lawyers?**
Yes, Spellbook is frequently used by contract managers and procurement teams.

**Does LegalOn integrate with CLM platforms?**
Yes, LegalOn integrates with Ironclad, SirionLabs, and other CLMs via API.
