# Clio AI vs MyCase: Which Practice Management Platform Is Best

Clio and MyCase have dominated the mid-market legal practice management space for over a decade. In 2026, both have evolved into AI-first platforms with generative drafting, intake automation, and predictive analytics baked in. Choosing between them comes down to workflow philosophy, pricing model, and which AI features you actually use.

## Quick Verdict

Clio AI is the better choice for growing firms that want the deepest feature set, a massive app marketplace, and aggressive AI roadmap. MyCase is the better choice for small firms that want an affordable all-in-one experience with client communication and payments built in. For solos, MyCase wins on price; for firms above 10 lawyers, Clio's ecosystem usually pays off.

## When to Choose Clio AI

Clio AI is the right pick when you want room to grow. Its marketplace ecosystem means you can plug in best-in-class tools for document assembly, e-signature, accounting, and marketing. Clio Duo's AI features cover drafting, summarization, and predictive time entry with growing sophistication. Firms planning to scale past 25 lawyers will appreciate Clio's enterprise-grade reporting and role-based permissions.

## When to Choose MyCase

MyCase is the right pick when you want an all-in-one solution with no assembly required. Because MyCase is owned by AffiniPay (which also owns LawPay), payment processing is deeply integrated. Solos and small firms love that they can run their entire operation on one subscription. MyCase IQ's AI features are competitive for drafting client communications and summarizing case files.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Clio AI** starts at $39 per user per month for the basic tier, scaling to $99 per user per month for the Elite plan that unlocks Clio Duo AI features, court rules, and advanced reporting. Add-ons for Clio Grow (intake) and Clio Payments run extra.

**MyCase** starts at $39 per user per month for Basic, $69 for Pro, and $99 for Advanced. MyCase IQ AI is included in Pro and above. LawPay payment processing is bundled.

## Clio AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Largest app ecosystem in legal tech
- Aggressive AI roadmap with Clio Duo
- Enterprise-grade reporting
- Strong API for custom workflows
- Excellent document automation

**Cons:**
- AI features gated behind highest tier
- Payments and intake are paid add-ons
- Steeper learning curve
- Can feel overbuilt for solos
- Higher all-in cost at scale

## MyCase Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Built-in LawPay payments
- Simple all-in-one pricing
- Great for solos and small firms
- Solid mobile app
- MyCase IQ included in Pro tier

**Cons:**
- Smaller app marketplace
- Limited advanced reporting
- Weaker document automation
- Less flexible API
- Fewer native court rules

## Final Recommendation

Solos and small firms focused on billable work and client communication should start with MyCase for its simplicity and bundled payments. Growing firms with complex workflows, multiple practice areas, or enterprise ambitions should invest in Clio AI. Both platforms offer free trials, so test-drive each with a real matter before deciding.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I migrate from MyCase to Clio?**
Yes, both platforms offer white-glove migration services. Expect 2-4 weeks for a mid-sized firm.

**Is Clio Duo included in the base plan?**
No. Clio Duo AI features are currently available only on the Elite tier.

**Does MyCase integrate with QuickBooks?**
Yes, MyCase offers native QuickBooks Online integration plus accounting exports.

**Which has better trust accounting?**
Both are competitive. Clio's trust accounting is slightly more robust for multi-state firms.

**Which AI is better, Clio Duo or MyCase IQ?**
Clio Duo currently leads on depth of features, but MyCase IQ is closing the gap quickly.
