# Lexis+ AI vs Westlaw Edge AI: The Ultimate Legal Research Showdown

Lexis+ AI and Westlaw Edge AI are the heavyweights of legal research. Both have been foundational to American legal practice for decades, and both have now integrated generative AI deeply into their research workflows. For most firms, the decision between them shapes daily lawyer life for years. Here is the complete breakdown.

## Quick Verdict

The choice between Lexis+ AI and Westlaw Edge AI often comes down to preference between Shepard's and KeyCite, plus which secondary materials your practice area depends on. Westlaw Edge AI currently leads on AI-assisted research polish thanks to its CoCounsel heritage. Lexis+ AI leads on global coverage and certain regulatory practice areas. Both are excellent; the right choice is firm-specific.

## When to Choose Lexis+ AI

Lexis+ AI is the right pick for firms deep in regulatory, healthcare, tax, or international practices. Matthew Bender treatises, CCH tax materials, and Lexis's global database make it the default for multinational regulatory work. Shepard's is also preferred by many appellate advocates for its signal clarity.

## When to Choose Westlaw Edge AI

Westlaw Edge AI is the right pick for US litigation and transactional practices. Practical Law's transactional know-how is unmatched, and KeyCite is the industry default for citation checking. Westlaw Edge AI also benefits from the CoCounsel generative AI engine, which currently leads the market in research accuracy and response quality.

## Pricing Breakdown

Both platforms use enterprise pricing in the $250-$600 per user per month range depending on bundled content. Firms with long-standing Westlaw or LexisNexis relationships often receive preferred pricing. A 50-lawyer firm typically spends $150,000 to $350,000 per year on either platform.

## Lexis+ AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Shepard's citator
- Matthew Bender and ALR depth
- Strong global coverage
- Deep regulatory materials
- Lexis Create drafting integration

**Cons:**
- Fewer AI features than CoCounsel
- Slower AI roadmap
- Occasional UX friction
- Smaller transactional know-how library
- Heavier enterprise sales cycle

## Westlaw Edge AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- CoCounsel-powered AI
- Practical Law know-how
- KeyCite industry standard
- Deep US coverage
- Rapid AI feature releases

**Cons:**
- Weaker international coverage
- Less depth on niche regulatory areas
- Higher cost at full bundle
- Enterprise sales overhead
- KeyCite signals less nuanced to some users

## Final Recommendation

For US-focused litigation and transactional practices, Westlaw Edge AI is the modern default thanks to Practical Law and CoCounsel integration. For global regulatory, healthcare, tax, and appellate practices, Lexis+ AI offers a better content library. Many firms subscribe to both for different practice groups, though consolidation pressure is real. If starting fresh in 2026, Westlaw Edge AI has the momentum.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is KeyCite or Shepard's more reliable?**
Both are industry-standard. KeyCite is slightly more widely used in brief writing; Shepard's is preferred by some appellate specialists.

**Can I use Lexis+ AI in the UK?**
Yes. Lexis+ AI covers UK law deeply and is popular among UK solicitors.

**Does Westlaw Edge AI include CoCounsel features?**
Yes, most generative AI features in Westlaw Edge AI are powered by the CoCounsel engine.

**Which is better for tax research?**
Lexis+ AI has the edge thanks to CCH tax materials integration.

**Can I switch without disruption?**
Migrations take 4-12 weeks depending on firm size and data portability needs.
