# Fastcase AI vs Casetext AI: Which Budget Legal Research Tool Wins

Fastcase AI and Casetext AI have long been the two leading alternatives to Westlaw and LexisNexis for budget-conscious lawyers. Both offer AI-assisted legal research at a fraction of the cost of the big two. In 2026, both have added generative AI features, but they still appeal to slightly different audiences. Here is how to decide.

## Quick Verdict

Fastcase AI is the right pick if you access it through a bar association membership (which most US state bars offer free or at low cost). Casetext AI is the right pick if you want a richer AI feature set and do not mind paying a subscription. For many solos, Fastcase is effectively free; for firms willing to invest, Casetext delivers more value per dollar.

## When to Choose Fastcase AI

Fastcase AI is the right pick if your state bar includes Fastcase in your membership, which is true in most US states. Vincent AI, Fastcase's generative research tool, has matured significantly and now covers natural-language research, document analysis, and brief summarization. The combination of free bar access and Vincent AI makes Fastcase nearly unbeatable on price for solos.

## When to Choose Casetext AI

Casetext AI is the right pick if you want richer AI features, particularly its skills library for research memos, timeline generation, and document review. Casetext's generative AI is more mature than Vincent AI for complex tasks, though its legacy database is narrower than Fastcase's global vLex-backed corpus.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Fastcase AI** is free through most state bar memberships, which dramatically tilts the economics. Direct subscriptions start around $95 per user per month for Premium, which unlocks Vincent AI and global content. **Casetext AI** starts around $110 per user per month for legacy plans. Firms must also factor in the value of secondary materials and citators.

## Fastcase AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Free via bar membership
- Global coverage via vLex merger
- Vincent AI maturing rapidly
- Affordable direct pricing
- Strong mobile app

**Cons:**
- Bad Law Bot citator less polished than Shepard's or KeyCite
- Smaller AI skill library
- Fewer secondary materials
- Less enterprise support
- Feature releases slower

## Casetext AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Deeper AI skills
- Mature document review
- Strong core CoCounsel heritage
- Solid civil practice coverage
- Good research accuracy

**Cons:**
- No bar association freebie
- Maintenance mode product
- US-only
- Weaker citator
- Sunset risk

## Final Recommendation

If your bar membership includes Fastcase, start there. You get credible AI-assisted research at zero marginal cost, and Vincent AI has closed much of the feature gap with paid competitors. If you need more advanced AI skills or document workflows, Casetext AI is worth the subscription, especially for small firms handling complex civil matters. Evaluate your bar benefits before paying for either.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Vincent AI included in bar access?**
Vincent AI is typically included at the Premium tier, which many bars offer as a paid upgrade. Some bars include Premium.

**Does Fastcase have international content?**
Yes, following the 2023 vLex merger, Fastcase now offers global content in 17+ jurisdictions.

**Is Casetext AI being sunset?**
Legacy Casetext AI is in maintenance mode. New development happens in CoCounsel at Thomson Reuters.

**Can I use both at once?**
Yes, many solos triangulate using Fastcase for primary research and a paid tool for advanced AI skills.

**Which has better mobile?**
Both have competent iOS and Android apps. Fastcase's mobile experience is slightly more polished.
