# Casetext AI vs Lexis+ AI: Which Legal Research Platform Wins in 2026

Casetext AI and Lexis+ AI represent the two dominant AI-first legal research platforms outside of Thomson Reuters' CoCounsel. Each is backed by a massive proprietary database and each takes a different approach to generative search, drafting, and summarization. This comparison explains the practical differences so you can pick with confidence.

## Quick Verdict

Lexis+ AI is the stronger choice for firms that already rely on LexisNexis content, need Shepard's citator, and want a polished enterprise product. Casetext AI is the better pick for solos and small firms seeking affordable AI research without the full LexisNexis subscription cost. Most mid-sized firms should benchmark both against CoCounsel before deciding.

## Head-to-Head Comparison Table

| Feature | Casetext AI | Lexis+ AI |
|---|---|---|
| Parent company | Thomson Reuters | LexisNexis |
| Primary database | Casetext (CARA) | LexisNexis full library |
| Citator | Limited | Shepard's |
| Secondary materials | Basic | Matthew Bender, ALR, AmJur |
| Starting price | ~$110/user/month | ~$250/user/month |
| Document drafting | Basic | Advanced with Lexis Create |
| Hallucination controls | RAG over Casetext | Shepardized grounding |
| Brief analysis | Standard | Advanced with argument check |
| Multi-jurisdiction | US federal and state | Global (US, UK, CA, AU, FR) |
| Deployment | Self-serve | Enterprise onboarding |
| Integration with MS Word | Limited | Deep via Lexis for Office |
| Mobile app | Yes | Yes |

## When to Choose Casetext AI

Casetext AI is the right call when you want AI research at an affordable price point and do not need access to LexisNexis secondary materials. It works best for civil litigators, plaintiffs' firms, and boutiques that primarily need federal and state case law plus basic statutes and regulations.

## When to Choose Lexis+ AI

Lexis+ AI is the right call when your practice depends on LexisNexis content. Tax, healthcare, insurance, and regulatory practices lean heavily on Matthew Bender treatises and ALR annotations, all of which Lexis+ AI can surface and cite inline. Shepard's is also the industry standard citator for many appellate practices and is indispensable for brief writing.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Casetext AI** is priced around $110 to $165 per user per month.

**Lexis+ AI** starts around $250 per user per month and can reach $600+ for full-stack bundles including Lexis+ content, Shepard's, and Lexis Create drafting. Existing LexisNexis customers typically receive discounts or bundle pricing.

## Casetext AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Affordable for solos and small firms
- Fast self-serve onboarding
- Good core skills
- RAG grounding on Casetext database
- Simple, intuitive interface

**Cons:**
- Limited secondary materials
- Weaker citator
- Maintenance mode product
- US-only jurisdictional coverage
- Thinner support

## Lexis+ AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Full LexisNexis database grounding
- Shepard's citator integration
- Deep secondary materials
- Advanced brief and argument analysis
- Global jurisdictional reach

**Cons:**
- More expensive
- Enterprise sales and onboarding
- Heavier UI with a learning curve
- Requires LexisNexis subscription for full value
- Slower feature release cadence

## Final Recommendation

For regulatory-heavy and appellate practices, Lexis+ AI is the better investment thanks to Shepard's and its treatise library. For general civil, criminal, and small-firm practice, Casetext AI offers a faster and cheaper path to AI-assisted research. Existing LexisNexis customers should upgrade to Lexis+ AI without hesitation. New buyers with no prior allegiance should trial both alongside CoCounsel to see which database best covers their practice area.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Lexis+ AI use Shepard's for citation verification?**
Yes. Every Lexis+ AI response with case law citations is grounded against Shepard's signals, dramatically reducing hallucination risk.

**Can Casetext AI cite Lexis-only content?**
No. Casetext AI is limited to its own database plus public primary law.

**Which is better for international practice?**
Lexis+ AI covers UK, Canada, Australia, and France natively, making it the clear choice for cross-border matters.

**Is Lexis+ AI worth the price jump from Casetext?**
For firms that already depend on LexisNexis content, absolutely. For generalists, probably not.

**Can I use both platforms?**
Yes, many firms run both to triangulate research, though the duplicate cost is rarely justified outside of large firms.
