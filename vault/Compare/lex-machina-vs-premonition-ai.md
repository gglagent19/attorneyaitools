# Lex Machina vs Premonition AI: Which Litigation Analytics Tool Wins

Lex Machina and Premonition AI are the two leading litigation analytics platforms. Both promise predictive insights into judges, opposing counsel, case outcomes, and settlement ranges, but they derive these insights from very different data sources. For firms building data-driven trial strategies, choosing the right platform matters.

## Quick Verdict

Lex Machina is the right pick for firms that want clean, structured federal and major state court analytics with polished dashboards and reliable outcome data. Premonition AI is the right pick for firms that want the broadest possible dataset, including state and local courts, and are willing to work with noisier data. Lex Machina wins on polish; Premonition wins on coverage.

## When to Choose Lex Machina

Lex Machina is the right pick for AmLaw litigation practices that rely on clean data and polished reporting. Its curated datasets are especially strong in intellectual property, antitrust, securities, and commercial litigation. Because Lex Machina is owned by LexisNexis, it integrates natively with Lexis+ AI for end-to-end research and analytics workflows.

## When to Choose Premonition AI

Premonition AI is the right pick for firms that need analytics on state trial court judges, local dockets, and niche courts that Lex Machina does not cover deeply. Premonition's data collection is more aggressive and covers jurisdictions where structured docket data is harder to find. This makes it invaluable for insurance defense, personal injury, and high-volume plaintiff firms that try cases in state courts.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Lex Machina** uses enterprise pricing that varies by practice area module and user count. A single-module subscription typically starts around $20,000 per year, scaling to six figures for multi-module deployments. **Premonition AI** starts around $15,000 per year with more flexible a la carte pricing for specific jurisdictions or judge packs.

## Lex Machina Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Clean, curated data
- Polished dashboards
- Native Lexis+ integration
- Strong IP and commercial coverage
- Reliable customer support

**Cons:**
- Weaker state and local court coverage
- Higher cost
- Slower to expand new practice areas
- Less flexible pricing
- Data refresh lag in some areas

## Premonition AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Broadest data coverage
- Strong state court analytics
- Flexible pricing
- Aggressive data collection
- Good for high-volume trial practices

**Cons:**
- Noisier data requires more scrubbing
- Less polished UI
- Limited Lexis integration
- Inconsistent coverage across jurisdictions
- Smaller support team

## Final Recommendation

For AmLaw firms primarily litigating in federal court, Lex Machina is the cleaner and more defensible choice. For plaintiff firms, insurance defense teams, and practices with heavy state court exposure, Premonition AI provides coverage that Lex Machina simply cannot match. Some firms license both, using Lex Machina for federal strategy and Premonition for state-level judge research.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Lex Machina cover state courts?**
Lex Machina covers select major state courts including Delaware Chancery and select California courts, but its federal coverage is deeper.

**How current is Premonition's data?**
Premonition updates most major dockets daily. Coverage and freshness vary by jurisdiction.

**Can either predict settlement values?**
Both offer settlement and outcome ranges based on historical data. Accuracy varies by practice area.

**Which integrates with Lexis+?**
Lex Machina integrates natively with Lexis+ as part of the LexisNexis family.

**Can a solo practitioner afford Premonition?**
Premonition offers more flexible pricing that can work for small firms, especially on a per-jurisdiction basis.
