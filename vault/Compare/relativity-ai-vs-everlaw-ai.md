# Relativity AI vs Everlaw AI: Which eDiscovery Platform Wins

Relativity AI and Everlaw AI are the two most influential eDiscovery platforms of 2026. Relativity has been the enterprise standard for over a decade; Everlaw has grown into a serious challenger with a more modern UX and native generative AI features. Both have integrated LLMs into document review, privilege detection, and deposition prep. Here is how they stack up.

## Quick Verdict

Relativity AI remains the enterprise standard for complex litigation, regulatory investigations, and multi-party matters. Everlaw AI wins on user experience, ease of deployment, and price-to-value for mid-sized matters. For solo litigators and boutique firms, Everlaw is almost always the better fit. For bet-the-company litigation, Relativity still leads.

## Head-to-Head Comparison Table

| Feature | Relativity AI | Everlaw AI |
|---|---|---|
| Deployment | Cloud or on-prem | Cloud only |
| Starting price | Enterprise ~$50K+/year | Per-matter + per-user |
| AI review | aiR for Review | EverlawAI Assistant |

| Deposition prep | Available | Strong |

| Multi-matter dashboard | Yes | Yes |
| Mobile access | Limited | Yes |
| Certification program | Relativity Certified | Everlaw Certified |
| Best for | Large firms, AmLaw | Mid-size, plaintiff, corporate |

## When to Choose Relativity AI

Relativity AI is the right call when your matter involves multiple parties, terabytes of data, and strict review protocols. Relativity's aiR for Review and aiR for Privilege products are battle-tested on the largest matters. Its ecosystem of certified partners, processing vendors, and analytics consultants is unmatched.

## When to Choose Everlaw AI

Everlaw AI is the right call when your team values UX, rapid onboarding, and transparent pricing. Everlaw's generative AI features, including document summarization, deposition outline generation, and narrative search, are deeply integrated and genuinely useful out of the box. Plaintiff firms and mid-sized corporate litigation teams especially love the mobile experience.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Relativity AI** uses enterprise pricing that varies with data volume, user count, and deployment model. Most mid-sized matters start around $50,000 with costs scaling based on data processed and hosted. **Everlaw AI** uses a simpler per-user plus per-gigabyte model, typically more affordable for matters under a few terabytes. Everlaw's pricing is usually 30-50% lower for equivalent scope.

## Relativity AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Enterprise scale and reliability
- Deep partner ecosystem
- aiR AI products proven on major matters
- Flexible deployment (cloud or on-prem)
- Extensive certification programs

**Cons:**
- Complex UI with steep learning curve
- Higher total cost
- Longer implementation cycles
- Legacy features can clutter UX
- Requires certified specialists

## Everlaw AI Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Modern, intuitive UI
- Fast deployment
- Transparent pricing
- Strong native generative AI features
- Great mobile experience

**Cons:**
- Cloud-only (not ideal for regulated environments)
- Smaller partner ecosystem
- Less proven on bet-the-company matters
- Fewer advanced analytics
- Smaller certification community

## Final Recommendation

For AmLaw 100 firms handling complex multi-district litigation, Relativity AI remains the default and safest choice. For mid-sized firms, plaintiff boutiques, and corporate legal teams managing their own investigations, Everlaw AI offers a better experience at a better price. Evaluate both with a real matter before committing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Everlaw handle terabyte-scale matters?**
Yes. Everlaw regularly hosts multi-terabyte matters and scales well on AWS.

**Is Relativity aiR for Review reliable?**
Yes. aiR for Review has been adopted by most AmLaw 100 firms and is validated for defensible review workflows.

**Which has better predictive coding?**
Both use active learning. Relativity's is more mature; Everlaw's is easier to configure.

**Can I run Everlaw on-prem?**
No. Everlaw is cloud-only (AWS).

**Which is better for government investigations?**
Relativity is more widely adopted by DOJ and SEC-facing firms due to its on-prem options.
