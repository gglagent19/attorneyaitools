# Clio AI vs Smokeball AI: Which Practice Management Tool Wins

Clio AI and Smokeball AI attack the practice management market from different angles. Clio is the horizontally broad platform with the largest ecosystem, while Smokeball is the vertically deep specialist known for automatic time tracking and form libraries. In 2026, both have layered generative AI on top of their core products. This comparison helps you decide which approach fits your firm.

## Quick Verdict

Smokeball AI is the better choice for small firms that want automatic time capture, rich document automation, and practice-area-specific form libraries out of the box. Clio AI is better for firms that prioritize flexibility, integrations, and a rapidly expanding AI feature set. Automatic time tracking is Smokeball's killer feature for billable-hour firms.

## When to Choose Clio AI

Clio AI is the right pick when flexibility matters more than opinionated workflows. Firms with unusual billing structures, custom intake processes, or specialized reporting needs will appreciate Clio's open API and massive app marketplace. Clio Duo's AI features are expanding rapidly and now cover drafting, summarization, and predictive time entry.

## When to Choose Smokeball AI

Smokeball AI is the right pick when your firm lives on billable hours and wants every minute captured automatically. Smokeball's desktop agent tracks time spent on emails, documents, and calls without manual start-stop. Combined with its 20,000+ form library, Smokeball is especially powerful for family law, estate planning, real estate, and immigration practices.

## Pricing Breakdown

**Clio AI** starts at $39 per user per month and scales to $99 per month for Elite (which unlocks Clio Duo). Add-ons for Grow and Payments run extra.

**Smokeball AI** starts at $59 per user per month for Bill and scales to $129 per month for Prosper+ which includes full AI, form automation, and advanced reporting.

## Clio AI Pros and Cons

- Flexible and customizable

- Rapid AI roadmap

**Cons:**

## Smokeball AI Pros and Cons

- Industry-leading form library
- Deep MS Word integration
- Live onboarding included
- Strong for opinionated practice areas

**Cons:**
- Higher starting price
- Smaller app marketplace
- Windows-first (limited Mac support)
- Less flexible API
- Narrower sweet spot

## Final Recommendation

If you run a family law, estate, or real estate practice and bill hourly, Smokeball AI almost pays for itself through recovered billable hours. For general civil practices, litigation boutiques, or firms that value ecosystem flexibility, Clio AI remains the safer bet. Run both trials with real matters before committing.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Smokeball work on Mac?**
Smokeball is primarily Windows-based. Mac support is limited to the web app, which loses the auto-time tracking feature.

**How accurate is Smokeball's automatic time tracking?**
Users report 85-95% accuracy. The desktop agent categorizes time by matter based on file and email activity.

**Can Clio Duo draft pleadings?**
Yes, Clio Duo can draft pleadings, correspondence, and memos based on matter data.

**Which has better e-signature?**
Both integrate with DocuSign and HelloSign. Clio's integration is slightly smoother thanks to its marketplace.

**Does Smokeball include trust accounting?**
Yes, trust accounting is included in all tiers.
