# ROSS Intelligence vs Casetext AI: Legal Research AI Compared

ROSS Intelligence and Casetext AI were both early pioneers of AI-powered legal research. Their histories intersected memorably when Thomson Reuters sued ROSS Intelligence over training data sourced from Westlaw, eventually leading to ROSS's 2020 shutdown. In 2026, ROSS has relaunched under new ownership with a rebuilt platform, while Casetext AI lives on as a legacy brand inside Thomson Reuters. This comparison focuses on the current state of each product.

## Quick Verdict

Casetext AI is the safer choice today thanks to its Thomson Reuters backing, mature Westlaw-adjacent grounding, and proven deployment history. The new ROSS Intelligence is worth watching but remains in an early growth stage. For firms that need production-grade legal research AI right now, Casetext is the pragmatic pick.

## When to Choose ROSS Intelligence

ROSS Intelligence is worth considering if you are an early adopter comfortable with a younger product and want to support an independent legal AI vendor. The relaunched ROSS has learned from its litigation history and uses carefully licensed data sources. Its UX is modern, and its pricing is competitive.

## When to Choose Casetext AI

Casetext AI is the right pick for firms that want a production-proven tool with Thomson Reuters support behind it. Its research accuracy is well understood, and its skills library covers core daily workflows. The trade-off is that Casetext is now a legacy product with limited future roadmap.

## Pricing Breakdown

**ROSS Intelligence** starts at approximately $89 per user per month, with higher tiers for additional features. **Casetext AI** starts around $110 per user per month. Neither is expensive by legal research standards, and both offer free trials.

## Final Recommendation

For firms that need a research AI tool in production today, Casetext AI is the lower-risk choice despite its legacy status. For firms willing to bet on an emerging alternative with a modern platform and independent roadmap, ROSS Intelligence is worth a trial. Most firms should also compare both to Paxton AI and Fastcase AI before deciding.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is ROSS Intelligence the same company that shut down in 2020?**
The brand and some IP were revived under new ownership. The current product is rebuilt from scratch with carefully licensed data.

**Is Casetext AI being discontinued?**
Thomson Reuters has not announced a hard sunset but has signaled gradual migration toward CoCounsel.

**Which has better research accuracy?**
Casetext AI has a longer track record. ROSS Intelligence's accuracy is improving but less well documented.

**Which has better document review?**
Casetext AI's skills library is more mature for document review tasks.

**Is ROSS Intelligence SOC 2 certified?**
ROSS Intelligence has pursued SOC 2 compliance in its relaunched form. Verify current certification status directly.
