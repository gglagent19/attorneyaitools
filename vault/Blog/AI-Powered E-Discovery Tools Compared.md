# AI-Powered E-Discovery Tools Compared

Electronic discovery remains one of the most expensive phases of litigation. When a case involves millions of emails, chat messages, documents, and files, the cost of human review can dwarf every other litigation expense. AI-powered e-discovery tools reduce that cost by 60 to 80 percent while improving accuracy and consistency.

In 2026, the e-discovery market offers several mature AI platforms. Here is how the leading options compare.

## [[Relativity AI]]

**Market position**: Industry leader with the largest user base

Relativity (formerly Relativity One in its cloud version) is the dominant platform in e-discovery, used by law firms, corporations, and government agencies worldwide. Its AI capabilities have expanded significantly.

### AI Features

- **Active learning**: Relativity's continuous active learning model ranks documents by relevance as reviewers code them, pushing the most important documents to the top of the review queue.
- **Clustering**: Groups conceptually similar documents together, allowing reviewers to make batch decisions rather than reviewing one document at a time.
- **Named entity recognition**: Automatically identifies people, organizations, dates, and financial amounts mentioned in documents.
- **Email threading**: Collapses email chains so reviewers only see the most inclusive message, eliminating redundant review.

### Strengths and Limitations

Relativity's ecosystem is its greatest advantage. Hundreds of integrations, a robust marketplace of add-ons, and a deep bench of certified consultants make it the safe choice for complex litigation. The downside is cost and complexity. Smaller firms may find the learning curve steep and the pricing challenging.

## [[Everlaw]]

**Market position**: Fast-growing challenger favored by litigation boutiques

[[Everlaw]] has built a loyal following among litigation-focused firms that value a clean interface and strong collaboration features. Its cloud-native architecture means there is no software to install and updates are continuous.

### AI Features

- **Predictive coding 2.0**: Everlaw's predictive coding uses a combination of supervised and unsupervised learning to classify documents with minimal reviewer input.
- **Storybuilder**: A unique feature that lets attorneys organize key documents into a narrative timeline, which is invaluable for trial preparation.
- **Context panels**: Shows related documents alongside the one being reviewed, helping reviewers understand context without switching screens.
- **Smart search**: Natural language search that understands legal concepts, not just keywords.

### Strengths and Limitations

Everlaw excels in usability and collaboration. Multiple parties can work in the same review workspace with granular access controls, making it ideal for multi-defendant litigation. Pricing is more transparent than Relativity, though still significant for large matters.

## [[Reveal AI]]

**Market position**: AI-first platform with advanced analytics

[[Reveal AI]] (formerly Brainspace) differentiates itself with a strong emphasis on AI analytics and visualization. It is particularly well-suited for investigations and regulatory matters where understanding communication patterns is as important as finding relevant documents.

### AI Features

- **Concept clustering with visualization**: Reveals themes and topics across a document collection using interactive visual maps.
- **Communication analysis**: Maps who communicated with whom, how frequently, and about what topics.
- **Sentiment analysis**: Identifies documents with emotional language that may indicate awareness of wrongdoing.
- **Multilingual support**: Analyzes documents in multiple languages without requiring translation.

### Strengths and Limitations

Reveal is the strongest option for investigations and compliance matters. Its visualization tools help attorneys and investigators see patterns that would be invisible in a linear review. It is less widely adopted than Relativity, which can be a concern for firms that need to collaborate with co-counsel already using a different platform.

## [[DISCO AI]]

**Market position**: Speed-focused cloud platform

[[DISCO AI]] emphasizes speed and simplicity. It was built from the ground up as a cloud platform and processes data faster than most competitors.

### AI Features

- **AI-assisted review**: Uses machine learning to suggest coding decisions based on reviewer patterns.
- **Automatic tag suggestions**: Recommends tags for documents based on content analysis.
- **Search analytics**: Shows search term hit rates and coverage statistics to help attorneys evaluate the completeness of their review.

### Strengths and Limitations

DISCO is the fastest platform for data ingestion and processing. Its interface is intuitive enough that attorneys can run their own reviews without heavy support from litigation support staff. However, its AI capabilities are less sophisticated than Relativity or Reveal for complex analytical tasks.

## Which Tool Should You Choose?

- **For large, complex litigation**: [[Relativity AI]] offers the deepest feature set and the widest ecosystem of integrations.
- **For collaborative, multi-party cases**: [[Everlaw]] provides the best collaboration tools and user experience.
- **For investigations and compliance**: [[Reveal AI]] has the most advanced analytics and visualization.
- **For speed and simplicity**: [[DISCO AI]] gets you from data collection to review faster than any other platform.
