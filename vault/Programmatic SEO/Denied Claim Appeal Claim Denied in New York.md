## Fighting a denied claim claim in New York, step by step

1. **Read the denial or estimate closely.** Pin down the exact policy provision your insurer leaned on for this denied claim claim.
2. **Document everything** in New York — dated photos, video, receipts, and a written timeline of the loss.
3. **Commission your own New York contractor estimate.** Where it exceeds the insurer's figure is exactly what you negotiate back.
4. **Request a re-inspection in writing** and submit an itemized rebuttal that ties each disputed item to your policy and your evidence.
5. **Use the formal channels:** a complaint to the New York Department of Insurance ([NAIC](https://content.naic.org/state-insurance-departments)) and, for valuation-only disputes, the appraisal clause.

> **Time limits matter here.** New York policies typically cap how long you have to act. Check your policy's deadline clause and the New York Department of Insurance so a technicality never closes your file.
