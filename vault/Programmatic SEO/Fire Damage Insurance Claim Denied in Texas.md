## Why Fire Damage Insurance Claims Get Denied in Texas

Across Texas, fire damage claims are denied or trimmed for a predictable set of reasons:

- Smoke and soot damage to unburned areas was excluded from the scope
- Contents (personal property) were valued at deep depreciation instead of replacement cost
- Additional living expenses (ALE) for temporary housing were underpaid or denied
- The cause or origin was disputed pending investigation

In Texas, where hailstorms, hurricanes, and freeze events drive a large share of property losses, fire damage claims are especially prone to causation disputes — insurers may attribute the damage to an excluded cause to reduce or deny payment.

## Fighting a fire damage claim in Texas, step by step

1. **Start with the paperwork.** Identify the precise clause or scope line behind the fire damage claim decision in Texas.
2. **Document everything** in Texas — dated photos, video, receipts, and a written timeline of the loss.
3. **Bring in a licensed Texas pro.** Their full scope routinely beats the adjuster's, and that difference is real money on a fire damage claim.
4. **Request a re-inspection in writing** and submit an itemized rebuttal that ties each disputed item to your policy and your evidence.
5. **Escalate** to the Texas Department of Insurance ([NAIC directory](https://content.naic.org/state-insurance-departments)); many policies also include an appraisal clause for valuation fights.

> **Deadlines are unforgiving in Texas.** Most policies set a contractual time limit to file suit (often one to two years) and require prompt notice of loss. Confirm the specifics for your policy with the Texas Department of Insurance — don't rely on a general figure.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I dispute a fire damage claim in Texas?**

Yes. A denial or low offer on a fire damage claim in Texas is the start of a negotiation, not the end. You can request a re-inspection, submit an itemized rebuttal, invoke your policy's appraisal clause, and escalate to the Texas Department of Insurance.

**Do I need a lawyer to fight a fire damage claim in Texas?**

Not always. Many Texas valuation disputes are resolved with a documented rebuttal or the appraisal process. A lawyer makes sense for outright coverage denials or bad-faith conduct. You can also run a free analysis first to see how large your gap is.

**How long do I have to appeal in Texas?**

Texas policies usually set a contractual deadline to file suit — commonly one to two years from the loss — plus a prompt-notice requirement. Check your policy's "suit limitation" clause and confirm with the Texas Department of Insurance.
