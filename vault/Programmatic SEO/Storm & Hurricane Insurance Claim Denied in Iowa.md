## Why Storm & Hurricane Insurance Claims Get Denied in Iowa

Across Iowa, storm and hurricane claims are denied or trimmed for a predictable set of reasons:

- Wind damage was reclassified as flood damage to push it outside the homeowners policy
- A separate (higher) hurricane or wind/hail deductible was applied
- The insurer argued damage pre-dated the named storm
- The scope omitted interior water intrusion that followed roof or window failure

In Iowa, where derechos, tornadoes, and hail drive a large share of property losses, storm and hurricane claims are especially prone to causation disputes — insurers may attribute the damage to an excluded cause to reduce or deny payment.

**How long do I have to appeal in Iowa?**

Iowa policies usually set a contractual deadline to file suit — commonly one to two years from the loss — plus a prompt-notice requirement. Check your policy's "suit limitation" clause and confirm with the Iowa Department of Insurance.
