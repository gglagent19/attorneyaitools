## Why Storm & Hurricane Insurance Claims Get Denied in California

Most storm and hurricane disputes in California come down to a handful of recurring tactics:

- Wind damage was reclassified as flood damage to push it outside the homeowners policy
- A separate (higher) hurricane or wind/hail deductible was applied
- The insurer argued damage pre-dated the named storm
- The scope omitted interior water intrusion that followed roof or window failure

In California, where wildfires, earthquakes, and mudslides drive a large share of property losses, storm and hurricane claims are especially prone to causation disputes — insurers may attribute the damage to an excluded cause to reduce or deny payment.

## Fighting a storm and hurricane claim in California, step by step

1. **Read the denial or estimate closely.** Pin down the exact policy provision your insurer leaned on for this storm and hurricane claim.
2. **Document everything** in California — dated photos, video, receipts, and a written timeline of the loss.
3. **Commission your own California contractor estimate.** Where it exceeds the insurer's figure is exactly what you negotiate back.
4. **Request a re-inspection in writing** and submit an itemized rebuttal that ties each disputed item to your policy and your evidence.
5. **Use the formal channels:** a complaint to the California Department of Insurance ([NAIC](https://content.naic.org/state-insurance-departments)) and, for valuation-only disputes, the appraisal clause.

> **Time limits matter here.** California policies typically cap how long you have to act. Check your policy's deadline clause and the California Department of Insurance so a technicality never closes your file.
