## Why Business Interruption Insurance Claims Get Denied in Louisiana

Across Louisiana, business interruption claims are denied or trimmed for a predictable set of reasons:

- The period of restoration was cut short, ending lost-income payments early
- Extra expense and payroll continuation were excluded
- The lost-income calculation used conservative revenue assumptions
- A covered physical-loss trigger was disputed

In Louisiana, where hurricanes and flooding drive a large share of property losses, business interruption claims are especially prone to causation disputes — insurers may attribute the damage to an excluded cause to reduce or deny payment.

## Turning a Louisiana denial around: the steps that work

1. **Start with the paperwork.** Identify the precise clause or scope line behind the business interruption claim decision in Louisiana.
2. **Document everything** in Louisiana — dated photos, video, receipts, and a written timeline of the loss.
3. **Bring in a licensed Louisiana pro.** Their full scope routinely beats the adjuster's, and that difference is real money on a business interruption claim.
4. **Request a re-inspection in writing** and submit an itemized rebuttal that ties each disputed item to your policy and your evidence.
5. **Escalate** to the Louisiana Department of Insurance ([NAIC directory](https://content.naic.org/state-insurance-departments)); many policies also include an appraisal clause for valuation fights.

> **Deadlines are unforgiving in Louisiana.** Most policies set a contractual time limit to file suit (often one to two years) and require prompt notice of loss. Confirm the specifics for your policy with the Louisiana Department of Insurance — don't rely on a general figure.
