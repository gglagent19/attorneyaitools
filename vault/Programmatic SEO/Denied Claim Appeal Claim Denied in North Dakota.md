## Why Denied Claim Appeal Claims Get Denied in North Dakota

## Fighting a denied claim claim in North Dakota, step by step

1. **Decode the denial.** Find the specific exclusion or scope item the adjuster cited on your North Dakota claim.
2. **Document everything** in North Dakota — dated photos, video, receipts, and a written timeline of the loss.
3. **Get an independent estimate** from a licensed North Dakota contractor — the gap between their scope and the adjuster's is your leverage.
4. **Request a re-inspection in writing** and submit an itemized rebuttal that ties each disputed item to your policy and your evidence.
5. **Take it higher** — file with the North Dakota Department of Insurance ([find it here](https://content.naic.org/state-insurance-departments)), or invoke your policy's appraisal provision for amount disputes.

> **Watch the clock.** Your North Dakota policy almost certainly has a "suit limitation" clause and a prompt-notice requirement. Verify both against your own contract and the North Dakota Department of Insurance before they cost you the claim.

**How long do I have to appeal in North Dakota?**

Yes. A denial or low offer on a denied claim claim in North Dakota is the start of a negotiation, not the end. You can request a re-inspection, submit an itemized rebuttal, invoke your policy's appraisal clause, and escalate to the North Dakota Department of Insurance.
