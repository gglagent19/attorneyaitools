## Why Storm & Hurricane Insurance Claims Get Denied in Michigan

Across Michigan, storm and hurricane claims are denied or trimmed for a predictable set of reasons:

- Wind damage was reclassified as flood damage to push it outside the homeowners policy
- A separate (higher) hurricane or wind/hail deductible was applied
- The insurer argued damage pre-dated the named storm
- The scope omitted interior water intrusion that followed roof or window failure

In Michigan, where severe storms and winter weather drive a large share of property losses, storm and hurricane claims are especially prone to causation disputes — insurers may attribute the damage to an excluded cause to reduce or deny payment.

## Fighting a storm and hurricane claim in Michigan, step by step

1. **Start with the paperwork.** Identify the precise clause or scope line behind the storm and hurricane claim decision in Michigan.
2. **Document everything** in Michigan — dated photos, video, receipts, and a written timeline of the loss.
