## WEEK 2 — EDUCATIONAL

### Day 8 — Monday
- Post type: How-to
- Target audience: Transactional attorneys
- Suggested time: 8:30 AM ET

How AI cuts contract review time by 70 percent without increasing risk.

The mistake most firms make is pointing AI at the final redline and hoping for the best. That is backwards.

Here is the workflow that actually works:

First pass: AI extracts all defined terms, obligations, and conditions into a structured summary. You read the summary, not the contract.

Second pass: AI compares the draft against your firm's standard positions and flags deviations. You review deviations, not boilerplate.

Third pass: AI checks cross references and internal consistency. You fix errors, not hunt for them.

Fourth pass: A human attorney signs off on risk, judgment, and client-specific strategy. That part does not get automated, ever.

The time savings come from flipping the default. Instead of reading every word and hoping you catch the issues, you go straight to the issues.

By 2028, the billable hour will not disappear. But it will stop making sense for 40 percent of the work firms currently bill by the hour.

The firms that win this transition will not be the ones that adopt AI first. They will be the ones that reprice their services first.

This is the conversation legal AI is forcing and most firms are still dodging.

### Day 26 — Friday
- Post type: Ethical lens
- Target audience: Ethics-minded attorneys
- Suggested time: 10:45 AM ET

A question I get asked often: is it ethical to use AI on client work?

Short answer: yes, when used with care. The ABA guidance and most state bar opinions have converged on a reasonable framework. Competence, confidentiality, supervision, and client communication are the four pillars.

The longer answer is that ethics in legal AI is less about whether you use the tools and more about how you verify their output, how you protect data, and whether you disclose appropriately to clients who care.

The attorneys getting into trouble with AI are not the ones using it. They are the ones using it without process. Adopt the tool, but also adopt the workflow that catches errors, tracks data flows, and documents decisions.
