# YouTube Pack — Shorts + Long-form

YouTube ranks in Google AND is the strongest known correlation with AI-search citations. Strategy: **1 Short/day** (reach) + **1 long-form/week** (authority). Repurpose Shorts to TikTok/IG.

CTA everywhere: "Free claim analysis — link in description" → `attorneyaitools.org/app?utm_source=youtube`

---

## 10 Shorts scripts (≤45s each; hook in first 1.5s)

**Format:** HOOK (0–2s) → VALUE (2–35s) → CTA (35–45s). Talk to camera or screen-record + voiceover.

1. **"Insurance denied your claim? Do this first."**
   Hook: "If your insurance claim got denied, do NOT call them back yet." → Value: get the denial reason in writing, find the exact policy clause, get an independent estimate. → CTA: "Free 90-second analysis — link below."

2. **"3 words that make adjusters reopen your claim."**
   Hook: "Three words that make insurance adjusters nervous:" → "Independent. Contractor. Estimate." Explain why the gap is leverage. → CTA.

3. **"They owe you money you don't know about."**
   Hook: "Your insurer is holding money you can legally claim." → recoverable depreciation explained (RCV vs ACV). → CTA.

4. **"Never cash this check."**
   Hook: "If your settlement check says 'full and final' — stop." → why cashing can close your claim; counter first. → CTA.

5. **"Adjuster lowballed you? Here's the tell."**
   Hook: "Here's how to know the adjuster lowballed you." → patch-not-replace, depreciation, missing code upgrades. → CTA.

6. **"Wind vs flood — the hurricane claim trick."**
   Hook: "After a storm, insurers play this trick." → reclassifying wind damage as flood to dodge coverage; how to push back. → CTA.

7. **"Public adjuster vs doing it yourself."**
   Hook: "Should you pay a public adjuster 10–20%?" → when it's worth it vs DIY with the right tools. → CTA.

8. **"How much is your claim REALLY worth?"**
   Hook: "The adjuster said $7,500. The real number was $20,300." → walk the gap (scope, code, O&P, depreciation). → CTA.

9. **"Your appeal letter in 60 seconds."**
   Hook: "Write an insurance appeal letter that actually works." → reference claim #, cite policy, itemize, attach proof. → CTA: "free template, link below."

10. **"Water damage denied as 'gradual'? Fight it."**
    Hook: "Insurer called your water damage 'gradual'? That's their move to deny." → sudden vs gradual; document the source. → CTA.

## 2 Long-form scripts (5–9 min)

### A. "I uploaded a denied insurance claim to AI — here's the $14,200 it found"
- **Intro (0–30s):** the problem — denied/lowballed claims, you're not stuck with the first number.
- **Demo:** screen-record uploading a (redacted) policy + adjuster estimate to attorneyaitools.org/app; show the gap it flags.
- **Teach:** explain each gap (ACV vs RCV, missing code, matching, O&P).
- **Action steps:** independent estimate → itemized rebuttal → appraisal/DOI.
- **CTA:** try the free analysis; disclaimer (self-help tool, not a lawyer).

### B. "Public Adjuster vs Lawyer vs DIY: which one for YOUR claim?"
- Break down cost (PA 10–20%, lawyer contingency 33–40%, DIY = your time).
- Decision table by claim size/type.
- Where AI-assisted DIY fits (flat fee vs % of recovery).
- CTA + link to the comparison guide.
